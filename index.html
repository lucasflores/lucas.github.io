Undergraduate Research in Heavy Ion Physics

Lucas Flores University of California Riverside (UCR PHENIX Collaboration) My research involves doing particle detector studies on a future detector which is to be implemented in the larger PHENIX detector housed at Brookhaven National Laboratory along the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. The new detector, the Muon Piston Calorimeter Extension (MPC-EX), is an extension to the existing MPC that will allow for fine-grained information on the development of electromagnetic showers. The MPC-EX is also capable of tracking minimum ionizing particles (charged hadrons) that do not shower in the detector. The studies I am doing are done in a detailed simulation of the detector that takes into account all materials and geometries. I am looking at how well we will be able to measure particle collision products known as Jets with the MPC-EX in order to better elucidate gluonic structure functions. This could possibly lead to new physics known as the Color Glass Condensate. Such studies are relevant to the design of novel and improved detection and imaging systems.
