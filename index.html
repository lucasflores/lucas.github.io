About Me

My name is Lucas Flores and I am currently an undergraduate at the University of California, Riverside majoring in both Physics and Mathematics. Currently I am doing research in the Heavy Ion group at UCR and you can read a bit more about that further down the page. Outside of school and research my interests lie in all around fitness, web development (of mostly this site for now), and occasional binge watching of TV shows online. Research

I am currently doing particle detector studies on a future detector to be implemented in the larger PHENIX detector housed at Brookhaven National Laboratory along the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. The new detector, the Muon Piston Calorimeter Extension (MPC-EX), is an extension to the existing MPC that will allow for fine-grained information on the development of electromagnetic showers, the MPC-EX is also capable of tracking minimum ionizing particles (charged hadrons) that do not shower in the detector. The studies I am doing are done in a detailed simulation of the detector that takes into account all materials and geometries. I am looking at how well we will be able to measure particle collision products know as Jets with the MPC-EX to better elucidate gluonic structure functions which could possibly lead to new physics known as the Color Glass Condensate. Such studies are relevant to the design of novel and improved detection and imaging systems.
